What Caused Global Warming?

What is climate change?

Many people’s understanding of climate change is based on the 2006 documentary "An Inconvenient Truth" (An Inconvenient Truth), that the phenomenon of Global Warming refers to greenhouse gases in the atmosphere and oceans. Excessive amounts (including carbon dioxide, methane, water vapor, and nitrous oxide) make the earth seem to be shrouded in a thick greenhouse, and the heat from the sun is difficult to dissipate, leading to temperature rises and triggering various extreme weather such as drought, rainstorms, and heat waves Wait.

Main reason that leads to Global Warming

The earth would originally absorb 49% of solar radiation after reflecting off the earth’s surface and the atmosphere. After the industrial revolution, mankind used fossil fuels in large quantities to produce many greenhouse gases of carbon dioxide, Absorbing long-wavelength radiation and greatly increasing the solar radiation absorbed by the earth is the so-called "greenhouse "Effect" is the cause of global warming.

Sources of CO2 emissions

  1. Power generation: most power plants use fossil fuels, after burning produce carbon dioxide.
  2. Transportation: When a vehicle is running, it emits carbon dioxide due to fuel.
  3. Industry: Mainly come from industrial activities in developed countries and release carbon dioxide.
  4. Buildings: use electricity and fuel to produce heating, cooling, lighting and power.
  5. Forests: The forests that have been cut down at present will reduce the storage capacity of carbon dioxide by 1/4.
